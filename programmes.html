CURRENT PROGRAMME

make_sense re_action education

The current economic crisis risks could increase youth unemployment by 600,000 this year. The issue of non-continuation in the UK before the outbreak is likely to be exacerbated. This is why we have teamed up with make_sense.org and the Laidlaw Foundation to take concrete action in the face of the coronavirus epidemic.

makesense is a non-profit global community that now has over 130,000 volunteers working on 8000+ local projects and is expected to grow larger. The makesense model is designed to train volunteers to take action locally and quickly. In response to the coronavirus pandemic, makesense has teamed with the Laidlaw Foundation to support the most vulnerable in society through the pandemic.

The Laidlaw Foundation invest in the education of the underprivileged and underrepresented in order to break the cycle of poverty, reduce inequality and develop a new generation of leaders.

As they put it, Even before the pandemic, the attainment gap between privileged and disadvantaged pupils (studied as the socioeconomic achievement gap) was shocking.(1)

The makesense and Laidlaw Foundation re_action programme is attempting to answer the following question “How do we support children, parents and teachers during the Covid-19 crisis to make sure children continue to learn and aspire?”
